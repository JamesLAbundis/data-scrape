In Oklahoma, it's worth noting that for Clinton, there is a slight positive correlation between income and fraction of votes, and a high negative correlation between counties with higher percentages of people 65 years and old and the fraction of vote. On the other hand, Bernie is fairly consistent in everything beside the percentage of whites where there is a noticable positive correlation. On the Republican side of things, Cruz beat Trump by a close margin on nearly every plot.
