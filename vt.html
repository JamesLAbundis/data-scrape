In Bernie's home state of Vermont, it's no surprise that he's very popular- he holds nearly 80+ percent of the fraction of votes in nearly every statistic observed. After Bernie, it's Trump that's the second most popular candidate, hovering at around 30-50 percent of the votes. Some other things to note: When graphing the average percentage of females per county to the fraction of votes, Trump's fraction increases signifigantly as the average percentage of females increases, compared to Hillary's, which stays relatively the same.
